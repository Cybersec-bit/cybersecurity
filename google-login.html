Beware of Phishing Websites

Phishing scams are a common way that cybercriminals compromise accounts. Being alert to phishing messages is a great way to protect yourself online. These scammers may try to compromise your business, workplace, or university accounts.

Scammers often use email, text messages, phone calls, and social media. Their goal is to scam people into giving away their personal information. They often pretend to be a person or organization you trust.

Don’t fall for phishing scams in two steps:

  1. First, check if it is a scam:
    • Know what to look for. View common types of phishing scams such as phishing emails and text messages.
    • Go directly to a source you can trust. Visit the official website, log in to your account, or call their phone number. Don't use the links or contact details in the message.
    • Check what the official source says about the details they might request from you. Often companies or government agencies will specify what they will and will not ask you online or over the phone. For example, if the bank says they will never ask for your password and someone claiming to be from the bank asks for it, it's likely a scam.
  2. Then, if you still think it’s a scam:
    • Don’t click on links, open attachments, or reply to requests. Scam messages may try to trick you into giving out personal information. A scammer might ask for your bank account details, passwords, or credit card numbers. They may also ask you to download files, software, or allow remote access to your computer.
    • Contact your bank. If you think your credit cards or bank account may be at risk, they may be able to close your account or stop a transaction.
    • Report a cybercrime via ReportCyber if you have fallen victim to a scam. Help keep others secure by reporting the scam to the National Anti-Scam Centre - Scamwatch.
